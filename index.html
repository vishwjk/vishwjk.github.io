About

Background

I am a Physics PhD candidate at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences (IMSc), Chennai. My work lies broadly in theoretical and mathematical physics, with a particular interest in how randomness, noise and rare events shape the evolution of physical systems.

During my PhD I have worked on a range of problems in stochastic dynamics and statistical physics, often motivated by questions around escape, relaxation and transport in complex energy landscapes.

I enjoy combining analytical calculations with numerical simulations, and I’m particularly interested in questions that connect rigorous mathematics with physically transparent intuition.

  • Affiliation: PhD in Physics, IMSc Chennai
  • Status: Completing PhD in the next 3–4 months
  • Interests: Stochastic processes, first-passage problems, non-equilibrium systems
  • Methods: Langevin/Fokker–Planck formalisms, analytical techniques, numerical simulations
Research

Current themes

A non-exhaustive list of topics I have been thinking about recently. You can adapt or expand this part as your research evolves.

  • Escape problems and mean first-passage times in complex energy landscapes, particularly in low-dimensional stochastic models.
  • Role of noise, dissipation and driving in non-equilibrium systems, with emphasis on rare-event statistics.
  • Connections between Langevin dynamics, Fokker–Planck equations and spectral properties of the underlying operators.
  • Design and analysis of simplified stochastic models that capture essential physics while remaining analytically tractable.
  • Numerical exploration of first-passage distributions, survival probabilities and escape rates in model systems.
